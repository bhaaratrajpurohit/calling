What is the 10% Buffer? — Read This First

⚡ Buffer = Your Safety Net Against Hidden Costs

When you add up all your costs — Twilio, AI platform, CRM — that is your calculated cost. But in real-world calling operations, your actual bill is always higher than what you calculated. The 10% buffer covers that gap.

Here is exactly what eats into your margin if you don't keep a buffer:

  • Failed call attempts that Twilio still charges: If a lead doesn't pick up, Twilio charges ₹1.26 per attempt anyway. 100 unanswered calls = ₹126 gone, silently.
  • Call rounding: Twilio bills per minute rounded up. A 2 min 10 sec call is billed as 3 full minutes. On 500 calls, this inflates your actual minutes by 15–20%.
  • LLM token overruns: When leads ask unexpected questions, the AI burns more tokens than a standard conversation. Your AI platform bill spikes.
  • Re-dials & retries: If a call drops mid-conversation (network issue), the system re-attempts — double billing on telephony AND AI for that call.
  • Voicemail detection time: The AI stays on the line for 10–15 seconds detecting a voicemail. That time is billed. 200 voicemails/month = ~40 billed minutes you didn't plan for.
  • Mid-month platform fee changes: Some AI platforms change their rates or add surcharges without prominent notice.

→ Bottom line: Always price with a buffer. 10% is conservative for real estate calling. You can use the slider below to adjust it between 5% and 25% based on your risk appetite.
